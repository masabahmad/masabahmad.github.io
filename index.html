I am a 3rd-year PhD student at the University of Connecticut advised by Omer Khan.

My research interests are in computer architecture and security , parallel computing, and scheduling. My research is about efficient architectural and system level parameters that can be scheduled onto many core machines such as multicores and GPUs. In this context, I have created a graph benchmark suite, CRONO, for analyzing parallel graph algorithms with various inputs and on multicores.

I have previously worked with The Naval Research Laboratory (NRL), where I analyzed and did efficient scheduling for path planning algorithms on GPUs. I am also part of an effort with Freescale Semiconductor (Now NXP), where I am helping with accelerating graph and big data workloads in shared memory and MPI multicore setups.

Projects.

  • CRONO, a graph benchmark suite for shared memory multicores (Best Paper Nominee at IISWC '15).
  • SAS, Currently developing a high level situationally adaptive scheduler (SAS ) that optimally schedules algorithms for input dependence, performance, and accuracy, on target architectures (multicores and GPUs).
  • HaRE, A parallel Hardware Redundant Execution scheme (HaRE ) that uses declarative resilience to selectively apply resilience schemes to both crucial and non-crucial code, while ensuring program correctness.
  • I also collaborate with Marten van Dijk on several security projects such as HaTCh and other secure architecture projects, such as M-MAP involving bounds checking and side channels.
